# HIRO -- Style Prompt

> Modern woodblock print -- the city stopped talking and nobody noticed

**TL;DR:** Flat illustration in the tradition of Japanese woodblock printing (mokuhanga), reduced to minimal compositional logic. Realistic but flat colors -- no gradients, no shading, no modeling. Visible paper grain, fine ink-weight linework, and subtle analog print artifacts: slight misregistration between color layers, occasional overlap bleed, and the imperfections of a hand-pulled relief print process. Extreme negative space. Mood is deadpan observational.

---

## Rendering Style

Flat color fields with no gradients, no shading, and no volumetric modeling. Every color reads as a separate block or layer, printed in sequence onto textured paper. Colors are naturalistic in hue but applied as solid unmodulated fills, the way a printmaker would ink a carved block. There is no attempt to simulate light, shadow, or three-dimensionality. A form is its color, and that color is uniform edge to edge.

## Print Process & Artifacts

The image should read as a physical print pulled from carved blocks, not a digital illustration. Key indicators:

- **Paper grain:** A fine visible texture (like washi or heavyweight matte stock) sits beneath all color. Even solid fills have subtle physical tooth. The paper is not white-white but a warm off-white.
- **Misregistration:** Color layers are very slightly offset from one another -- by fractions of a millimeter. This is nearly imperceptible but visible on close inspection, especially where a color field meets a different-colored background. Edges don't align perfectly. A shape may have a hairline gap of paper showing along one side and a hairline overlap of color on the other.
- **Overlap bleed:** Where two color blocks meet, there is occasionally a thin strip where both inks overlap, producing a slightly darker or muddied edge. This is not consistent -- some edges are clean, some show it.
- **Ink density variation:** Flat color fields are not perfectly uniform. There may be very subtle variation in ink saturation across a large area, as if the block was not inked with absolute evenness. This is extremely subtle -- more felt than seen.
- **Registration marks are absent.** These are finished prints, not proofs.

These artifacts should be understated. The overall impression is of a skilled printmaker working with care, not of a sloppy process. The imperfections are evidence of analog craft, not error.

## Linework

Fine, even-weight, ink-black lines used sparingly for interior detail only. Lines are never used as outlines around major shapes -- large forms are defined by the edge where one color field meets another. The lines sit on top of the color layers (printed last) and are the sharpest, most precisely registered element in the image. Line weight is consistent and thin -- comparable to a fine technical pen or a sharply carved key block.

## Color Application

Colors are naturalistic in hue but flat in application. Each color is a single unvarying tone with no attempt at atmospheric perspective, highlight, or shadow. The palette per image is limited (typically 3-5 colors) simply because the subjects tend to be simple, not as a stylistic constraint on hue. If a scene required more colors, they would be added as additional printed layers.

## Composition & Framing

Radical negative space. The subject occupies a small portion of the frame, surrounded by large unbroken color fields. Objects are placed off-center, often in the upper third or along one edge, leaving the remaining canvas empty. Compositions are asymmetric and use the tension between the occupied area and the empty field as the primary visual dynamic. Framing is cropped and observational -- as if glanced at while passing. Vertical formats dominate. No ground plane is shown in vertical compositions -- objects float against open field.

## Mood & Narrative Tone

Still, dry, observational. The images have the quality of a long pause -- looking at something ordinary and holding the gaze for a beat too long until it becomes compositionally interesting. No drama, no narrative, no movement. Time is suspended. The humor, when present, is deadpan -- delivered entirely through composition rather than expression or exaggeration.
