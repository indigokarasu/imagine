# NOIR -- Style Prompt

> Twilight insomnia in a city that never fully darkens

**TL;DR:** Flat vector illustration, no outlines. Three-color palette only: coral-vermillion (#FF574C), olive-gray (#858470), and near-black (#141414). Backlighting from an oversized low sun. Silhouetted foregrounds against hazy atmospheric depth. Film-still composition with strong horizontal banding. Mood is quiet urban melancholy -- still, solitary, warm-toned but emotionally distant.

---

## Color Palette

Strictly limited to three color channels plus black:

- **Coral-vermillion** (#FF574C): the dominant chromatic force, ranging from #EE5945 in reflected glow to #FF574C at full intensity. Used for sky glow, reflected light on surfaces, interior light through openings, and highlights on edges. Warm but not blood-red -- it leans toward fluorescent coral.
- **Warm mid-gray** (#858470, ranging from #565B54 in shadow to #8A8070 in open haze): atmospheric haze, distant forms, fog. This is the "air" color -- everything between the viewer and the horizon takes on this desaturated olive-khaki tone.
- **Near-black** (#141414, ranging from #0D0D0D to #1A1A18): all foreground silhouettes, structural elements, figures. Functions as the primary drawing color.
- No blue, green, yellow, or white. If a surface would logically be another color, it is rendered as a value shift within the coral/gray/black triad.

## Lighting & Atmosphere

A single dominant light source positioned low on the horizon, enormous relative to the scene (occupying 20-30% of the frame width). All light radiates from this source. It produces strong backlighting, turning all foreground elements into silhouettes. Atmospheric perspective is heavy -- forms lose contrast and detail with distance, dissolving into the warm gray haze. Interior light, when visible through openings, glows the same coral (#FF574C) as the sky. There is a subtle grain or noise texture across all surfaces, as if printed on matte stock with visible tooth.

## Rendering Style

Flat vector-influenced illustration with minimal gradients. Forms are built from solid color shapes layered front-to-back, not from line work. There are no outlines or contour lines -- shapes are defined purely by value contrast between adjacent color blocks. Where detail exists, it is achieved through negative space cuts in silhouette shapes rather than drawn lines. Brushwork is absent -- edges are clean and hard, except for the light source itself which has a soft, diffused halo bleeding into the surrounding haze. The grain texture is applied globally as a post-process, not per-element.

## Composition & Framing

Panels are composed like film stills or storyboard frames. Strong horizontal banding: sky occupies the upper register, mass fills the middle, and a dark foreground element anchors the bottom. Depth is communicated through value layers -- darkest elements (#141414) closest, lightest elements (#858470) farthest, with the coral light source (#FF574C) as the focal depth marker. Vertical elements create a lattice that the light filters through. Figures, when present, are shown in profile silhouette, positioned at frame edges, never centered. Scale contrast is important: the light source is massive, figures are small, the environment is dense and indifferent.

## Mood & Narrative Tone

Quiet melancholy without despair. The dominant emotion is stillness at the threshold of day and night -- everything pauses, the figure watches, nothing happens but the light changes. There is no violence, no action, no drama -- only atmosphere. Loneliness as a neutral state, not a crisis. The oversized light source functions as both beauty and weight -- it dominates everything, inescapable and indifferent.
