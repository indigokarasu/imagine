# SOMA -- Style Prompt

> Thermal imaging of a memory you're not sure you had

**TL;DR:** Ethereal, heavily blurred digital painting. Subjects rendered as soft luminous forms with no edges, lines, or detail. Palette is a continuous gradient field of peach (#FFC8AA), lavender (#8A8BC6), coral-pink (#F39F90), soft cyan (#4AB7D2), and mint green (#89C9A4). No background/foreground distinction -- color IS the form. Mood is warm, dissolving, pre-verbal.

---

## Rendering Style

Extreme gaussian blur applied to every element. No hard edges exist anywhere in the image. Forms are defined entirely by soft color transitions -- gradients that shift over tens or hundreds of pixels rather than discrete boundaries. The technique resembles heat-map visualization or infrared thermography rendered in pastel. There are no outlines, no contour lines, no texture, no brushstrokes, no surface detail. Subjects are recognizable by silhouette shape alone, and even silhouettes dissolve at their margins into the surrounding color field. The brightest areas (near-white #FCF9F2) glow from within, as if the subject is emitting light rather than reflecting it.

## Color Palette

Colors operate as continuous gradient fields, not discrete fills. Multiple hues blend into one another within a single form:

- **Peach-apricot** (#FFC8AA to #FFD4B7): the dominant warm ground tone, often filling the majority of the canvas
- **Lavender-periwinkle** (#8A8BC6 to #5B69C1): used for depth and background atmosphere
- **Coral-salmon** (#F39F90 to #FD7E5E): warm accents, interior glow, emotional heat
- **Soft cyan-teal** (#4AB7D2 to #479ACC): cool counterpoint, used for open space or ambient atmosphere
- **Mint-seafoam green** (#89C9A4 to #BBD1A3): appears in transitional zones where warm and cool meet
- **Rose-pink** (#E691AE to #EC96BB): secondary warm tone, blending between coral and lavender
- **Near-white** (#FCF9F2): the hottest point -- center of luminous subjects, glowing outward

Colors never appear as flat fills. Every area of the image contains at least two hues in soft transition. The overall effect is iridescent -- shifting between warm and cool depending on where you focus.

## Light & Luminosity

There is no external light source. Subjects appear to generate their own light from within, brightest at their core (#FCF9F2) and cooling toward their edges into the surrounding atmosphere. This creates an effect like bioluminescence or subsurface scattering. The background is not "lit" -- it simply exists as a color field that the subject's glow bleeds into. There are no cast shadows, no reflections, no specularity. Brightness = proximity to the subject's center.

## Form & Figure Treatment

Subjects are reduced to their most essential silhouette. All detail is dissolved. Faces have no features -- occasionally the faintest suggestion of a closed eyelid rendered as a slightly warmer color shift. Bodies are volumes of color, not structures of anatomy. The level of abstraction is comparable to seeing a form through frosted glass or remembering it with your eyes closed.

## Composition & Framing

Simple, centered or slightly off-center compositions. One subject per image. The subject occupies 40-70% of the frame, surrounded by open color field. Framing is intimate. Square or near-square aspect ratios. No environment, no ground plane, no horizon. The subject exists in a dimensionless color space. Negative space is not empty -- it is an active part of the color gradient, continuous with the subject.

## Mood & Narrative Tone

Pre-verbal tenderness. The images feel like the visual equivalent of being half-asleep -- forms are present but not fixed, warm but not sharp, familiar but not specific. There is no narrative, no action, no context. Just a being, softly luminous, held in color. The emotional register is gentle and unguarded -- closeness without detail, recognition without identification.
