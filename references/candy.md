# CANDY -- Style Prompt

> A painting you can't quite focus on, interrupted by color that doesn't explain itself

**TL;DR:** Soft painterly landscape (airbrush/blended oil, uniformly out of focus) overlaid with crisp, opaque geometric color fields (coral #FF6B4F, teal #13AC8A, cerulean #0F9FD3, acid yellow #E1D147). Two separate visual planes -- soft behind glass, sharp on the surface. Warm parchment (#FBEBD4) surround. Classical plein air composition underneath. Fine matte grain over everything. Mood is serene, suspended, neither present nor absent.

---

## Era & Temporal Register

Temporally ambiguous. The landscape layer is rendered with the soft competence of mid-20th-century plein air illustration. Floating over it are hard-edged geometric color fields with no historical anchor. Neither layer dominates. The image exists in the overlap between traditional painting and contemporary graphic design, but does not reference or depict any specific technology, interface, or device.

## Color Palette

Two distinct palettes coexist on separate visual planes:

**Background (landscape) palette:**
- Water: desaturated steel-blue (#9EAFC3), shifting toward muted mauve (#AFA1A3) in shadow
- Foliage: olive-chartreuse (#ADBE66) in highlights, deep olive-brown (#746949) in shadow
- Stone and rock: warm sand (#D0B084) to pale cream (#F7DCBE)
- Sky: warm parchment white (#FBEBD4), never reaching true blue
- Blossom accents: muted pink, tiny and sparse

**Foreground (color fields) palette:**
- Coral-red (#FF6B4F to #FE633C): saturated and fully opaque, the most aggressive accent
- Teal-green (#13AC8A): vivid, used sparingly
- Cerulean blue (#0F9FD3 to #3E8CB4): ranging from deep saturated to mid-tone
- Gradient fills blending coral-to-peach and green-to-yellow within individual shapes
- Yellow-chartreuse (#E1D147 to #D6CA41): acid-bright
- All color fields are fully opaque with no transparency into the scene behind them

## Rendering Style

The background is painted with soft, blurred brushwork -- every edge is slightly out of focus as if viewed through frosted glass or a tilt-shift lens. Forms are rendered with volumetric softness: rounded, light wrapping around surfaces, no hard edges anywhere in the landscape. The technique suggests airbrushing or heavily blended oil paint -- visible brushstrokes are absent. There is a slight overall bloom or glow, as if overexposed by half a stop.

The foreground color fields are pill-shaped or rounded-rectangle forms with mathematically consistent corner radii, flat fills or smooth linear gradients, and crisp edges that contrast sharply against the soft background. These shapes cast no shadows onto the landscape and receive no light from it -- they exist on a separate visual plane entirely, hovering over the painting like physical color swatches laid on glass.

## Depth & Focus

The entire background operates at a single shallow depth of field -- uniformly soft, with no element in sharp focus. Near and far elements have roughly equal blur. This creates a dreamlike flatness despite naturalistic perspective. The color fields, by contrast, are perfectly sharp and appear to hover at the viewer's focal plane. The effect is looking through a pane of glass: the landscape is behind it and slightly out of reach, the color shapes are on its surface.

## Composition & Framing

The landscape follows classical plein air composition: framing elements on left and right edges, an opening to depth in the center, gentle recession from foreground to distance. The color fields are scattered across this composition with casual asymmetry -- some left-aligned, some right, staggered vertically, varying in width. They partially occlude the scene but never enough to destroy its readability. The overall frame has softly rounded corners and sits on a pale warm-white surround (#FBEBD4).

## Surface & Texture

A fine uniform grain or noise sits over the entire image -- both landscape and color fields -- unifying them into a single physical surface. This grain is subtle, more like matte photo paper tooth than digital noise. The landscape has a slightly waxy, smooth quality. The color fields are perfectly smooth within their boundaries but share the same global grain overlay.

## Mood & Narrative Tone

Serene suspension. The landscape is beautiful but inaccessible -- too blurred to inhabit, too soft to touch. The color fields are vivid but empty -- pure sensation without meaning. Together they produce a feeling of pleasant floating: the eye moves between soft depth and hard surface, warm scene and cool geometry, without settling. Nothing demands attention. Nothing resolves.
